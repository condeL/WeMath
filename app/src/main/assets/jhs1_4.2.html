3.2 Comparing and ordering integers 

We can compare two different integers by looking at their positions on the number line. For any two different places on the number line, the integer on the right is greater than the integer on the left. Note that every positive integer is greater than any negative integer. These numbers are shown in Fig. 4.4. For instance –7 < –5, since the mark for –7 is to the left of the mark for –5.

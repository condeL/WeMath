Representing fractions on the number line

In Chapter 1 of this book, we learnt about how to represent natural numbers on the number

line. We can also represent factions on the number line. Fig. 3.7 shows part of the number

line with some fractions located on it.

When we divide the interval between 0 and 1 into three equal parts, we obtain the points A and B as shown in Fig. 3.7. A and B therefore corresponds to the fractions 1/3 and 2/3 respectively on the number line. Similarly, when the second unit interval, between 1 and 2, is divided into three equal parts and one part of them is taken, starting from 1 and moving towards 2, we locate the points C and D which corresponds to the fractions 13/1 and 23/2 respectively. 

Types of fraction 

Proper Fraction: In a proper fraction the numerator is less than the denominator, as in, 2/3, 1/2 ,etc. 

Improper Fraction: In an improper fraction the numerator is greater than the denominator, as in, 35/8 , 52/3 etc. 

Mixed Fraction: A mixed fraction contains both a whole number and a fractional part, as in, 33/2, 43/2, etc. 

Like Fraction: Like fractions are fractions with a common denominator. For example, 2/7, 3/7 are like fractions.

Unlike Fraction: Unlike fractions have different denominators. For example, 2/3,  3/7 are unlike fractions.

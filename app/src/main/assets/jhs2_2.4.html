2.4 Using Differentiation with Graphs 

In section we saw that finding the tangent to a function is the same as finding its slope at a particular point. The slope of a function at a point is just its derivative. If we want to find a general formula for a tangent to a function, we differentiate the function. To find the slope of the tangent at a particular point, we substitute that point’s x value into the function’s derivative. This will give us a single value, which is the slope of a straight line.

1.0 Numbers and Numerals


1.1 Letters and Arithmetic


When you add, subtract, multiply or divide two numbers, you are performing arithmetic. 

These four basic operations (+, −, ×, ÷) can be performed on any two real numbers. 

Since they work for any two real numbers, it would take forever to write out every possible combination, since there is an infinite amount of real numbers!

To make things easier, it is convenient to use letters to stand in for any number, and then we can fill in a particular number when we need to. 

For example, the following equation:


x + y = z


In this equation, 'x' represents the amount of change you should get z. 'z' is the amount you payed and 'y' is the price of the item. 

All you need to do is write the amount you payed instead of z and the price instead of y, your change is then x. 

But to be able to find your change you will need to rearrange the equation for x.

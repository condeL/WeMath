2.2.1 Percentages 

Percentages such as 60%, 45 % and 25% are recognized as rational numbers.

Example 

60% = 60/100  =  3/5,  

45% = 45/100 = 9/20, 

25% =  25/100 = 1/4 

2.2.2 Irrational numbers 

Numbers such as p, 2, 3, 5 etc. cannot be expressed as ratios of two integers. Such numbers are called irrational numbers. Irrational numbers have no termination and are nonrecurring. Example: 2 = 1.414 213 562 … 3 = 1.732 050 808 … 5 = 2.236 067 977 … p (pi) = 3.141 592 654 … The set of irrational numbers is denoted by Q¢.
